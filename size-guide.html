HOW TO MEASURE YOUR WRIST

Step-by-step guide to get accurate measurements

1

Use a Flexible Measuring Tape

Wrap a flexible measuring tape around your wrist just below the wrist bone. This is where your watch will naturally sit.

2

Alternative: Use String

If you don't have a measuring tape, wrap a string around your wrist, mark the overlap, then measure the string with a ruler.

3

Consider Your Preference

Add 5-10mm to your measurement if you prefer a loose fit, or subtract 2-3mm for a snug fit.

Measurement Tips

Measure at the end of the day when wrist is largest
Don't pull the tape too tight or too loose
Measure both wrists - they may differ slightly
Round to the nearest millimeter for accuracy

SIZING FAQ

Common questions about watch sizing

What if I'm between sizes?

If you're between sizes, consider your lifestyle and preferences. For active wear, choose the smaller size. For a statement piece, go larger.

Can I adjust the bracelet?

Yes, metal bracelets can be adjusted by removing or adding links. Leather straps come in different lengths and can be shortened if needed.

Do complications affect sizing?

Complications like chronographs add thickness and visual bulk. Consider this when choosing your size, especially for dress watches.

What about women's watches?

Women's watches typically range from 26-36mm. Consider your wrist size and personal style preferences when choosing.
